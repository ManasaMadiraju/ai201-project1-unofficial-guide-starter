SOURCE: The Santa Clara (Student Newspaper) — "Food Insecurity Remains a Continual Challenge at Santa Clara University"
URL: https://www.thesantaclara.org/blog/food-insecurity-remains-a-continual-challenge-at-santa-clara-university

At SCU (tuition exceeding $80,000/year), nearly 28% of students experience food insecurity per the 2023-2024 Annual Assessment of Food Security and Basic Needs. This is down from 35% in 2022, but still affects roughly 1 in 4 students at one of the most expensive universities in the country.

SCALE: University enrolled 9,178 students in 2023-2024.

COST DISPARITY ON CAMPUS:
Research from the Agroecology, Climate Resilience, and Food Justice Lab revealed students struggle to afford nutritious meals both on and off campus. One student noted: "A pound of apples might cost $1.29 or $1.99 at bigger stores. On campus, one apple will cost like $3."

SIMPLY OASIS ISSUES:
The SimplyOasis allergen-safe dining station (opened 2023) has not adequately served students with celiac disease. Students report it frequently lists dairy as an ingredient and lacks clear gluten information — defeating its purpose for students with serious dietary restrictions.

UNIVERSITY RESPONSES:
- Hired a Basic Needs Coordinator
- Established a Basic Needs Committee
- Bon Appétit launched a Value Menu with options under $11
- Bronco Pantry (campus food pantry) exists but student awareness remains limited
- Students may now donate unused dining points to peers experiencing food insecurity

IMPACT:
Food insecurity affects "mental health, academic performance, and overall student success." Students on tight budgets report skipping meals, choosing cheaper off-campus food (sometimes less nutritious), or relying on the Bronco Pantry when they know about it.

TIPS FOR STUDENTS:
- Ask about the Value Menu at Benson (options under $11)
- The Bronco Pantry is free and available to all SCU students — location varies by quarter, check the Basic Needs website
- You can donate dining points to other students if you have surplus at end of quarter
- Dining Plus points (purchased separately) are tax-free and carry over year-to-year until graduation
