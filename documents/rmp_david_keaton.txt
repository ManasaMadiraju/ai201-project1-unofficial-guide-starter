Rating: 3.5/5 | Difficulty: 3/5 | Would Take Again: 63%

REVIEW 1 (ENGL1A, Apr 2, 2026) — Quality 4, Difficulty 1, Grade A:
"You don't know your grade unless you ask him. Really chill guy is all I have to say. Overall, good prof if you aren't super into English. Not a lot of writing in 1A but expecting more for 2A. You only get participation points if you actively speak in class often and he checks you off on a sheet, not just showing up."

REVIEW 2 (ENGL1A, Jan 14, 2026) — Quality 5, Difficulty 3, Grade A:
"Great class with a lot of classroom conversations about current events. Requires reading and watching a lot of crime shows. If you read and watch what's assigned you'll do fine."

REVIEW 3 (ENGL1A, Jan 14, 2026) — Quality 2, Difficulty 4, Grade A-:
"He wasn't clear about grading and how you were doing in the class. It ended up as a surprise at the end of the quarter. Also, his grading isn't very quantifiable and is very hard to meet A standards."

REVIEW 4 (ENGL171, Jan 1, 2026) — Quality 5, Difficulty 3, Grade A:
"It was a little annoying to have to write everything in class but you get used to it. And this was the only creative writing class I've taken here that wasn't full of people turning in old work or AI written stuff."

REVIEW 5 (ENGL1A, Dec 25, 2025) — Quality 2, Difficulty 2, Grade B:
"Skipped many classes, and graded everything a week after finals. Did not give written feedback on essays."

SUMMARY: Professor Keaton teaches ENGL1A and ENGL171 (Creative Writing) at SCU. Students describe him as chill and low difficulty, but grading transparency is a recurring complaint — you often don't know your grade until the end. Participation (active verbal contribution) is tracked and required for full credit. More lenient than Carroll for the writing core requirement.
