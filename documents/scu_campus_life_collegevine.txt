STUDENT ORGANIZATIONS:
Approximately 150 student organizations at SCU, spanning academic clubs, cultural organizations, athletic teams, and recreational groups. Associated Student Government (ASG) organizes year-round events including concerts, club fairs, and community gatherings.

HOUSING PROGRESSION:
- First-year students: traditional residence halls with structured programming (RAs, floor events, orientation activities)
- After freshman year: students can apply for university villas, on-campus apartments, or communal-style halls
- Many upperclassmen move off-campus to the surrounding Santa Clara/San Jose neighborhoods

COMMUNITY AND VALUES:
Strong emphasis on volunteer work and community involvement, rooted in SCU's Jesuit mission. Many students describe their time at SCU as personally transformative not just academically but through service learning, social justice engagement, and ethical development. Core courses (like Ethics in the Digital Age, Literacy and Social Justice) reflect these values.

LOCATION ADVANTAGES:
Silicon Valley location provides exceptional professional and cultural opportunities:
- Internship and job recruiting from major tech companies (Amazon, Microsoft, Google are top recruiters)
- Access to diverse cultural events across the Bay Area
- San Francisco is about 45 minutes away by car/Caltrain
- Santa Cruz beaches accessible for weekend trips
- Hiking in the Santa Cruz Mountains nearby

NOTABLE TRADITION:
Grand Reunion Weekend each fall: class dinners, brunches, alumni parties, and tailgate events before football games. One of the most anticipated annual events on campus.

GREEK LIFE:
Fraternities and sororities are a significant part of the social scene, particularly for weekend events. Not mandatory to join to have a social life, but Greek organizations host many of the larger campus parties and events.

CLASS SIZE AND ACADEMIC EXPERIENCE:
Average class sizes of 20-25 students for freshmen. This enables meaningful faculty-student relationships. Professors provide assigned office hours and career guidance. Not impacted like larger UC schools — students generally get into the courses they need.

POST-GRADUATION:
- 77% of graduates placed within 6 months of graduation
- Top employers: Amazon, Microsoft, Google
- Notable alumni: Leon Panetta (former Secretary of Defense/CIA Director), Steve Nash (NBA All-Star), Khaled Hosseini (author of The Kite Runner), Janet Napolitano (former Arizona Governor)
