HOW DINING POINTS WORK:
1 dining point = $1. There are no "meal swipes" — all purchases deduct points from your balance. This means no wasted swipes for missed meals, but you must budget carefully because points can run out.

2025-26 DINING PLAN OPTIONS (points per quarter):
- Basic Plan: 2,172 points (~$2,172/quarter)
- Preferred Plan: 2,541 points (~$2,541/quarter)
- Junior/Senior Plan: 1,491 points (~$1,491/quarter)
- Apartment Plan: 326 points (~$326/quarter)
- Graduate Plan: 326 points/quarter
- Law Plan: 489 points/semester

FIRST-YEAR RESTRICTIONS:
First-year students living in the residence halls can ONLY select the Basic Plan or Preferred Plan. You cannot opt for the lower Junior/Senior or Apartment plans as a freshman.

CARRYOVER RULES:
- Up to 100 unspent resident dining points carry over to the next quarter if you remain in on-campus housing
- This means up to $100 in unused points aren't lost between quarters

DINING PLUS (supplemental points):
- Can be purchased separately if you run out of plan points
- Tax-free purchases (saves ~9% in sales tax)
- Unused Dining Plus points carry over year-to-year until graduation
- Non-refundable and non-transferable

STUDENT TIPS:
- Track your balance — dining points have a per-day average you should stay near (~$24/day on Basic Plan)
- If you have surplus points near end of quarter, spend them at Benson or The Cellar Market, or donate to peers
- Dining Plus is worth buying if you consistently overspend — the tax savings add up
- Off-campus Flex partners (Safeway, Ike's, Pizza My Heart) accept dining points and can be cheaper per meal than on-campus options
- The Value Menu at Benson has options under $11 — useful for budget-conscious students

COST CONTEXT (2025-26 housing + dining):
- Standard Double room + Basic dining plan = ~$21,207/year
- Suite Single room + dining = ~$22,962/year
- Apartment Single + dining = ~$23,991/year
