SOURCE: The Santa Clara (Student Newspaper) — "Top 10 Santa Clara Professors"
URL: https://www.thesantaclara.org/blog/top-10-santa-clara-professors

This list was compiled by the SCU student newspaper and reflects student opinion on the best professors at Santa Clara University.

1. LAURIE POE (Mathematics)
Listed among Princeton Review's "Best 300 Professors." Presents material clearly to ensure no student falls behind. Students praise her ability to make complex math accessible.

2. DR. STEPHEN CARROLL (Writing / Critical Thinking and Writing)
"Stephen Carroll will rip your writing apart and force you to stitch it back together." — Kathryn Hackett, senior
Known for demanding, unconventional writing instruction that permanently changes how students write.

3. MICHAEL LASLEY (English — Critical Thinking and Writing)
Maintains a friendly attitude that comforts first-year students and encourages college engagement. Seen as a gentler alternative for the CTW core requirement.

4. SAM CONEDERA, S.J. (Medieval History)
"Made me ecstatic to learn about the crusades...turned it into the best class I've taken." — Ryan Mahoney, senior
A Jesuit priest who brings passion and humor to medieval history.

5. MICHAEL ZAMPELLI, S.J. (Theater / Jesuit Mission)
Energetic and passionate; represents university spirit; embodies the Jesuit belief in educating the whole person — intellectually, spiritually, and practically.

6. DR. SALLY VANCE-TREMBATH (Theology)
Encourages personal reflection on religion; shows genuine care for students as "companions." Makes theology feel personally relevant rather than academic.

7. DR. LAWRENCE NELSON (Ethics / Philosophy)
Demanding instructor who challenges students because he recognizes their potential. Students leave his classes with substantially stronger analytical writing skills.

8. ALDO BILLINGSLEA (Theater — Acting for Non-Majors)
Creates fun, energetic classes requiring full participation. One of the most popular electives for non-theater students at SCU.

9. DR. FRANCISCO JIMÉNEZ (Spanish)
Author of the autobiography "The Circuit." Embodies SCU's three pillars: competence, conscience, and compassion. Students describe his class as life-changing.

10. DR. ANTHONY HAZARD (History)
"He opened up my eyes to the diverse world we truly live in." — Will Mittenthal, student
Known for bringing diverse global perspectives into historical analysis.

NOTE: This list was published by The Santa Clara, SCU's independent student newspaper. It reflects student nominations and editorial judgment at time of publication.
